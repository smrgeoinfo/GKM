GSSP defined at the base of a silty mudstone layer (within bed 9) 52.8m above the base of the Kaili Formation in the Wuliu-Zengjiayan section in Jianhe County, eastern Guizhou Province, China. This level coincides with the first appearance of the cosmopolitan oryctocephalid trilobite Oryctocephalus indicus. Secondary indicators near the GSSP include the ROECE negative delta 13C excursion, the simultaneous appearance of acanthomorphic acritarchs, and a transgressive phase of a major eustatic event. The Wuliu-Zengjiayan section is situated along a hill ridge which is about 0.5km northeast of Balang Village, Jianhe County, GuizhouProvince, China at a latitude of 26.74738333 N and a longitude of 108.4138333 E. The GSSP is located inside the Jianhe Natural Reserve of Paleontological Fossils and the Miaoling National Geopark.
